Local Ensemble Flow Filters for Large-scale Dynamic Systems

J. A. F. Magalhães, O. B. L. Neto, I. Harjunkoski, F. Corona
Under review in Journal of Process Control
Sequential Monte Carlo methods State estimation Reaction–diffusion systems

Pathwise Approximations to Solving the Filtering Problem for the Stochastic Chemostat

J. A. F. Magalhães, M. F. Emzir, F. Corona
Under review in Results in Applied Mathematics
Stochastic processes Splitting methods Pathwise filtering

We establish existence, uniqueness, and non-explosivity of solutions to the stochastic differential equations governing the chemostat model. We then compare several pathwise approximations for solving the associated filtering problem.

Sparse Grids for the Weighted Poisson Equation in Particle Filtering-based Estimation

J. A. F. Magalhães, M. F. Emzir, I. Harjunkoski, F. Corona
Under submission
Sequential Monte Carlo methods PDEs Hyperbolic-cross approximations

Particle flow methods require solving transport equations that can become computationally expensive in high-dimensional settings. This work investigates sparse grid approximations for the weighted Poisson equation arising in particle filtering and establishes theoretical error bounds for the proposed numerical approach.

Gaussian Processes with Logistic Mean Function for Modeling Wind Turbine Power Curves

G. Virgolino, C. Mattos, J. A. F. Magalhães, G. Barreto
Renewable Energy, 162:458–465, 2020.
Machine learning Renewable energy

This work proposes a probabilistic model for wind turbine power curves based on Gaussian processes with a logistic mean function. The approach improves the representation of nonlinear power generation behaviour while providing uncertainty estimates that are useful for renewable energy applications.

Sparse Spectral Methods for Approximating PDE Solutions in Particle Flow

J. A. F. Magalhães, M. F. Emzir, F. Corona
SIAM Conference on Control and its Applications (CT25), Montreal, Canada, 2025.
Particle flow Numerical methods

This work develops sparse spectral approximations for solving the partial differential equations underlying particle flow methods. The proposed approach aims to reduce the computational cost of particle transport while maintaining accurate approximations for high-dimensional state estimation problems.

Block Particle Filters for State Estimation of Stochastic Reaction–Diffusion Systems

J. A. F. Magalhães, O. B. L. Neto, F. Corona
IFAC World Congress, Yokohama, Japan, 2023.
State estimation Stochastic systems

We apply block particle filtering for the state estimation of stochastic reaction–diffusion systems. The proposed approach exploits the local structure of spatially distributed systems while analysing the role of importance sampling strategies in improving accuracy.
